## Post Body

> Hey GrowthHackers,
>
> We all know the standard SaaS SEO playbook: hire an agency or writer, research keywords, build content briefs, write, format, audit readability, publish to WordPress, index, and check GA4.
>
> It’s a highly repetitive, operationally heavy loop that takes hours of manual coordination every week.
>
> To solve this for our startup, we built an end-to-end **autonomous SEO agent loop** (MeetLyra). The agent handles the entire lifecycle on autopilot:
>
> 1. **Auto-Discovery:** Pulls live Google Analytics & Search Console signals to locate declining URLs or near-ranking keywords.
> 2. **Intent Research:** Runs real-time SERP scans to construct comprehensive briefs.
> 3. **Drafting:** Writes long-form (2,000-word) Markdown copies.
> 4. **Pre-Publish Audits:** Automatically audits Flesch Readability (checks sentence limits under 15 words) and Yoast compliance before publishing.
> 5. **Rich Block Construction:** Injects Custom HTML table-of-contents, FAQs with schema, and workflow infographics.
> 6. **CMS Publish & IndexNow:** Publishes to WordPress and pings Search Console / Bing indices instantly.
>
> We just documented the full blueprint and the exact architecture behind our swarms. You can check out the step-by-step setup guide here:
>
> `https://blog.meetlyra.app/autonomous-seo-content-engine/`
>
> Would love to hear your thoughts on agentic content loops—are you guys letting agents write and publish autonomously, or do you still keep a heavy editor-in-the-loop?
